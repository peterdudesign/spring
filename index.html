Spring Cleaning Tips for Residents

Learn how to get an organized and spotless home with our guide.

July 27, 2023

Spring is a wonderful time to clean your home and get rid of any clutter, dust, and stains that have accumulated over the winter. Here are some handy spring cleaning tips for Toronto Community Housing residents to make your cleaning simpler and more productive.

Make a plan and a checklist

Before you start cleaning, it’s helpful to have a clear goal and a list of tasks to complete. This will help you prioritize the areas that need the most attention and avoid getting overwhelmed or distracted.

Getting rid of clutter

To make your home more spacious and tidy, you should start your spring cleaning by eliminating clutter. Clutter can create a chaotic appearance, waste precious room, and interfere with your cleaning process. Examine each room and divide your belongings into three categories: keep, donate, and toss. Give away or recycle the things that are still usable but you don’t want or need anymore. Dispose of the things that are broken, ruined, or outdated.

Gather your supplies and equipment

Before you start, gather all the essential cleaning supplies, equipment, and accessories. This will help you work faster and avoid distractions while you clean. You may prefer to use natural or homemade cleaners, such as vinegar, baking soda, and lemon juice, to reduce toxic substances and cut costs. For example, you can use a mixture of vinegar and water to clean glass surfaces, and lemon juice to degrease appliances.

Here are some of the items you’ll need:

  • Bucket
  • Garbage bags
  • Clean microfiber cloths
  • Reusable spray bottle
  • All-purpose cleaner
  • White distilled vinegar
  • Baking soda
  • Dish soap
  • Duster
  • Vacuum cleaner
  • Rags or Mop

Clean all appliances

A spotless kitchen in a Toronto Community Housing unit

Kitchen appliances can get dirty and greasy over time. To keep them in good shape, you should clean them regularly with these simple methods.

Refrigerators: Remove the shelves and drawers and wash them with soapy water. Then clean the inside of the refrigerator with a mix of water and baking soda.

Ovens: Use baking soda and vinegar to deep clean your oven.

Stovetops: Wipe down your stovetop once a week with hot, sudsy water.

Microwaves: Wipe down the inside of the microwave with a cloth.

Small Appliances: Follow the manufacturer’s instructions for cleaning your small appliances. In general, you can wipe them down with a damp cloth and dish soap, and dry them off with a soft cloth or paper towel.

Spring cleaning can be a rewarding and enjoyable activity that can improve your health, mood, and well-being. By following these tips, you can make your Toronto Community Housing home shine this spring!
